**Status:** Active

## Summary

Core functional movements that build lifelong strength, mobility, posture, and resilience. These six essential movements support health across your lifespan.

## Core Functional Movements

### 1. Overhead Dumbbell or Barbell Press (Vertical Push)

**Benefits:**
- Builds shoulder, triceps, and upper chest strength
- Improves shoulder mobility and stability
- Promotes posture and bone density

**Tip:** Keep ribs down, avoid back arch, full range of motion

### 2. Pull-Ups (Vertical Pull)

**Benefits:**
- Strengthens back, biceps, and grip
- Balances pushing work to protect shoulders
- Improves posture and pulling ability

**Tip:** Use assistance bands or negatives if needed

### 3. Barbell or Dumbbell Seal Rows (Horizontal Pull)

**Benefits:**
- Strengthens upper back (rhomboids, traps, lats)
- Improves posture and shoulder health
- Counters forward shoulder rounding

**Tip:** Pull with control; avoid jerking or using momentum

### 4. Push-Ups (Horizontal Push)

**Benefits:**
- Builds chest, shoulders, triceps, and core stability
- Joint-friendly pressing movement
- Functional strength for daily activities

**Tip:** Keep body straight; avoid hips sagging

### 5. Goblet Squat

**Benefits:**
- Improves lower body strength and mobility
- Builds strong legs and glutes while protecting joints
- Enhances hip and ankle range of motion

**Tip:** Keep heels down, chest tall, full depth as comfortable

### 6. Lunges

**Benefits:**
- Builds single-leg strength, balance, and coordination
- Reduces asymmetries and improves stability
- Strengthens glutes, quads, and core

**Tip:** Focus on control, not speed. Step back or forward comfortably

## Optional: Aesthetic & Balance Work

### Lateral Raise

**Benefits:**
- Builds shoulder width and stability
- Improves posture and shoulder joint balance
- Supports shoulder aesthetics

**Tip:** Use light weight and slow control

### Strict E-Z Bar or Dumbbell Curls

**Benefits:**
- Strengthens biceps and supports elbow health
- Enhances grip and pulling strength
- Adds arm size and symmetry

**Tip:** Keep elbows fixed, avoid swinging

### Overhead Rope Extension

**Benefits:**
- Strengthens triceps and elbow stability
- Balances pressing muscles
- Improves arm tone and shape

**Tip:** Control both stretch and squeeze; elbows stay still

## Programming Principles

### Frequency & Volume

- Train each movement pattern 2x per week
- 3-4 sets per exercise
- Rest adequately between sessions (48 hours for same muscle group)

### Progression

- Start with bodyweight or light weight
- Focus on perfect form first
- Progress weight gradually when form is solid
- Consistency matters more than heavy loads

### Integration

Include these movements in a broader training program:
- 2-3 sessions per week minimum
- Combine pushing and pulling movements
- Add lower body work for balance
- Recovery (sleep, nutrition) is critical
