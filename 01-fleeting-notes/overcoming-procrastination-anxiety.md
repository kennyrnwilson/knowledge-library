# Overcoming Procrastination and Anxiety: A Comprehensive Guide

**Status:** Fleeting Note
**Created:** 2025-11-01
**Source:** Notion Fleeting Notes

## Summary

Dealing with procrastination and anxiety requires a multi-faceted approach integrating psychological strategies, physiological understanding, and philosophical insights. Procrastination relates to motivation which can be boosted by maximizing expectancy and value while minimizing impulsiveness and delay. Anxiety is a force that shrinks your world through avoidance, but can be addressed through hierarchical exposure therapy and Stoic philosophy.

## Procrastination Strategies

### The Motivation Equation
**Motivation = (Expectancy × Value) / (Impulsiveness × Delay)**

To boost motivation:
- **Maximize Expectancy:** Your belief in succeeding and receiving the reward
- **Maximize Value:** How much you enjoy the task or its reward
- **Minimize Impulsiveness:** Your tendency to get distracted
- **Minimize Delay:** Time until you receive the anticipated reward

### Practical Tactics

1. **Break Down Tasks** - Divide into smaller, manageable steps to boost expectancy
2. **Keep Tasks Small & Just Start** - Use Pomodoro (25-min blocks), focus on starting
3. **Set the Bar Low** - Deliberately set initial goal lower than capability (boosts expectancy)
4. **Hack Pleasure from Experience** - Reward yourself immediately after completion
5. **Use Parkinson's Law** - Work expands to fill time; create artificial deadlines
6. **Deliberate Environment** - Eliminate distractions, minimize impulsiveness
7. **Understand Your Personality** - Self-awareness is key to optimizing your habits

### Leverage Dopamine (Huberman)
- **Steepen the Dopamine Trough:** Engage in something harder than your current amotivated state
- **Micro-Sucks & Macro-Sucks:** Regularly do undesired tasks to activate AMCC (brain region for tenacity)
- **Make Effort the Reward:** Cultivate mindset where effort itself becomes rewarding
- **Protect Intrinsic Motivation:** Be cautious about over-rewarding activities you already enjoy

## Anxiety Management

### Hierarchical Exposure Therapy (Dr. Scott Eilers)

1. **Identify Zones:**
   - **Comfort Zone:** No or very little anxiety
   - **Overwhelming Zone:** Extreme anxiety, panic attacks, undoable
   - **Exposure Therapy Zone:** Manageable anxiety with coping skills

2. **Engage in Exposure Therapy Zone** - Builds comfort zone, shrinks overwhelming zone
3. **Utilize Coping Skills** - Deep breathing, mindfulness, thought challenges, sensory tools
4. **Adjust Variables** - Social support, time/day to find "just right" exposure level

### Stoic Strategies (Ryan Holiday)

1. **Problems are a Feature:** Accept challenges as gifts for growth
2. **Negativity Won't Matter in 3 Months:** Most worries are forgotten quickly
3. **Learning Comes from the Edges:** Radical change arises from severe challenges
4. **Stop Taking Things So Seriously:** Life is finite and ridiculous—be liberating
5. **Process Your Emotions:** Explore, observe, meditate on emotions; use journaling
6. **Suffer More in Imagination:** Most worries never happen—focus on present
7. **No One Can Harm You Without Consent:** Harm is a choice; control your narrative
8. **Walking & Physical Activity:** Essential for mental rest and gratitude
9. **Dichotomy of Control:** Focus intensely on what's in your control
10. **Is This Essential?:** Routinely eliminate the inessential

### Physiological Management (Dr. Eric Berg)
- **Balance Mental Stress with Physical Work:** Exercise shifts focus and aids sleep
- **Stay in Action:** Avoid paralysis and withdrawal from worry/fear
- **Key Nutrients:** Vitamin B1 (anti-stress), Magnesium & Potassium (relaxation), Vitamin D (mood)
- **Dietary Approaches:** Keto and intermittent fasting increase parasympathetic control
- **Adaptogens:** Herbal supplements for stress coping
- **Breathing & Sleep:** Equalize inhalation/exhalation; protect sleep quality

### Addressing Conflict (Alan Watts)
- **Embrace Authentic Conflict:** Honest expression of authentic self when encountering resistance
- **Overcome Fear of Loss:** Your true value comes from authenticity, not accommodation
- **Be Unmovable in Who You Are:** Maintain essential nature like a river or mountain
- **Practice Small Acts:** Start with minor honest self-expression, build capacity

## How I'll Use This

Build procrastination-beating habits by breaking tasks small and starting immediately. For anxiety, practice exposure therapy in manageable increments and apply Stoic principles to reframe worries as temporary and manageable.
