# Swimming

**Purpose:** Guidance for swimming activities and aquatic exercise.

## Overview

Swimming is an excellent full-body, low-impact activity for fitness, recreation, and wellbeing.

## Benefits

- **Full-Body Workout** - Engages all major muscle groups
- **Low-Impact** - Easy on joints compared to running
- **Cardiovascular** - Excellent aerobic and anaerobic training
- **Flexibility** - Improves range of motion and mobility
- **Mental Health** - Stress relief and meditative benefits
- **Accessibility** - Suitable for various fitness levels and ages

## Getting Started

1. **Find a Pool** - Local community pool, gym, or beach
2. **Learn Proper Technique** - Consider lessons for efficiency
3. **Start Gradually** - Build endurance progressively
4. **Consistency** - Regular practice yields best results

## Swimming Workouts

### Beginner
- 20-30 minutes, 2-3x per week
- Mix swimming with walking/treading water
- Focus on technique over distance

### Intermediate
- 30-45 minutes, 3-4x per week
- Structured workout with intervals
- Mix of different strokes

### Advanced
- 45-60+ minutes, 4-5x per week
- High-intensity interval training
- Competitive or endurance focus

## Key Strokes

- **Freestyle** - Most efficient, good for distance
- **Backstroke** - Good for posture and back strength
- **Breaststroke** - Easier to learn, good for fitness
- **Butterfly** - Most challenging, great for power
